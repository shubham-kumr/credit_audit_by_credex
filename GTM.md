# GTM.md — SpendLens Go-To-Market

## Exact Target User

**Engineering Team Lead at a 15-50 person Series A startup**, typically titled "Engineering Manager", "VP Engineering", or "Head of Engineering". The company has been using AI coding tools for 6-18 months and the stack has grown organically — individuals expensed tools, the team grew, and now there are 3-5 overlapping subscriptions that nobody has audited.

This person doesn't own the finance function but is asked by the CFO or founder every quarter to justify the AI tooling budget. They know they're probably overpaying somewhere but don't have time to go through vendor pricing pages. They're embarrassed to bring a vague "we should audit our AI tools" to a finance meeting — they need a defensible number.

Secondary target: **CTOs at bootstrapped product studios (5-15 people)** who wear the finance hat themselves and feel the $200-400/month AI tool bill directly.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days, $0 Budget

**Week 1 — Warm distribution (Days 1-7):**
1. Post in Rands Leadership Slack #tools channel: "Built a tool that audits your AI subscriptions — found $345/month for a 5-person team. Free, 2 minutes, no login. Feedback welcome." Share the link. Target: 20-30 users.
2. DM 20 engineering managers directly on LinkedIn with a message that shows the specific finding relevant to their company size ("Most 10-person teams I've talked to are paying for both Cursor and Copilot — here's a free tool that checks that for you").
3. Post to HackerNews Show HN on a Tuesday morning. Target: 50-200 upvotes, 20-40 users from HN alone if the framing is specific ("Show HN: Free audit tool for AI tool subscriptions — found overlap in 70% of audits").

**Week 2 — Communities (Days 8-14):**
4. Post in Cursor Discord #general: "Made a quick tool to check if you're actually saving money with Cursor vs keeping Copilot — useful if your team runs both." Non-promotional framing, direct relevance to the community.
5. Post in r/ExperiencedDevs: "I built this after spending 30 minutes on vendor pricing pages — it takes 2 minutes and found $345/month for my team."
6. Write a short LinkedIn article: "We were paying $390/month for AI tools with a 5-person eng team. Here's what the audit found." Personal framing performs better than product pitches on LinkedIn.

**Week 3 — Leverage (Days 15-21):**
7. Email every person who completed an audit and saved >$200/month: "Would you be open to a 10-minute call? I'm trying to understand what changed after your audit." These become testimonials and referrals.
8. Reach out to 5 developer-focused newsletters (TLDR, Software Lead Weekly, Pointer.io) with a short note. These have high eng manager readership and often feature free tools.
