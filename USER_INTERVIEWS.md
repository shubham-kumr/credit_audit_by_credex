# User Interviews - SpendLens

Two informal discussions with university seniors and close professional LinkedIn connections conducted during development week. Each conversation lasted 10-15 minutes.

---

## Interview 1 - Ashwani K., Software Engineer (IIT Mandi Alumnus)

**Date:** May 22, 2026
**Connection:** University senior and LinkedIn connection.

**Background:** Ashwani is a Full-Stack/AI Engineer who has previously interned at IIT Mandi and HopeFloats. In his software engineering team, different AI tools like Cursor, GitHub Copilot, and ChatGPT were adopted organically by developers, but the team's total licensing spend was never tracked or audited.

**Direct Quotes:**

> "We are paying for Cursor, Copilot, and ChatGPT Team all at once. I honestly have no idea who is using what. When our team was asked about our AI tooling budget last month, we just said we were still testing. In reality, we just haven't had the time to check."

> "I do not care about saving $50 a month as much as I care about having a quick, defensible answer when justifying software costs. The real value is having a clean page to show leads that justifies our exact spending."

**Key Insight:** The savings amount itself was less important to Ashwani than having a shareable, credible report that he could use to justify their development choices.

**Action Taken:** We prioritized the shareable slug URL as a major feature. The results page copy was changed from "Save results" to "Share with your finance lead" to match this job.

## Interview 2 - Trishant Rajora, Founder @CybersecurityGhost

**Date:** May 23, 2026
**Connection:** Professional LinkedIn connection building India's first AI-powered cyber ecosystem.

**Background:** Trishant runs a fast-growing, early-stage cybersecurity firm. Running a specialized startup means every dollar in software spend matters, especially when managing multiple Cursor and Claude seats for developer workflows.

**Direct Quotes:**

> "We literally share a Claude account to avoid the $125 per month minimum on the Team plan. I know it is not ideal, but per-seat pricing gets expensive quickly for small teams."

> "My problem is not that I do not know we are overpaying. It is that I do not have 30 minutes to sit down, open five different pricing pages, and calculate the cheapest option. I just need a tool that tells me exactly what to downgrade to."

**Key Insight:** Vague suggestions do not work. Trishant needed direct, actionable commands like "Switch from Claude Team to two separate Claude Pro accounts to save $85 per month."

**Action Taken:** We refined the audit outcome descriptions to recommend highly specific plan downgrades and seat changes rather than generic cost-cutting advice.
